Folk Art

Hungary is rich in artistic expression through various forms. Intricate patterns emerge, whether it be embroidery, woodcarvings, or pottery. The designs are often colorful and decorative.

Embroidery

Each region has their own embroidery design. Things such as pillowcases, tablecloths, and clothes are embroidered. In the past, women and girls would embroider after a long day's work or to pass time in the winter. They would adorn special costumes with the region's pattern.

Easter Egg Decorating

Hungarian Easter eggs are colored and dyed in many ways. These include scraping the design into a colored egg, using wax to draw on the egg, using leaves to create a pattern, and wrapping it in a thread. This can take many hours.

Gingerbread Decorating

The frosting on a decorated Hungarian gingerbread cookie mimics intricate lace and the colorful floral embroidery patterns found on folk costumes. They are often given as gifts. Heart shaped gingerbread cookies dyed red often have a small mirror on them. In the past, people would give these to their loved ones, signifying that they are in their heart.

Lace Making

Hungary has a specific type of lace unique to it. Halasi Csipke or "Lace of Halas" is a famous type of needle lace that was developed in the town of Kiskunhalas, Hungary in 1902. This type of needlework became a rival to the lace of Brussels and Venice in the 20th century.

Felting

Felting has been used since the arrival of the Hungarian people in the Carpathian Basin in the year 896. It is used to make things such as hats, carpets, purses, and pillowcases. Felt can be shaped and formed easily in different shapes and dyed different colors.

Leather Working

Leather can be designed in many ways. It is often burned, cut, stamped into, or perforated. Using these methods, products such as belts, purses, saddles, and holsters are created and decorated.

Wood Working

Wood carving is an ancient Hungarian art. In the past, wood workers would not only carve intricate designs, but craft beds to bowls. In Szeklerland, many people have an ornately carved wooden gate as the entrance to their property.

Pottery

The two main types of Hungarian pottery are Korondi and Haban. Both Korondi and Haban pottery date back to the 16th century. Korondi pottery comes from the town of Korond, which used to be a part of Hungary, and is now a part of modern day Romania.
