Musical Styles by Region

Transdanubian

To the west of the Danube, which runs north to south through the middle of Hungary, there is yet again a different variety of music. Some instruments like the citera, the bagpipe, and the hurdy-gurdy give this music its typical sound.

  • Trandanubian Music
  • Music from Sarkoz
  • Somogyi Songs

Tisza/Great Hungarian Plain

Along the Tisza River in Eastern Hungary, spanning from the north to the south, different sounding music emerged characterized by more upbeat rhythms and the use of the cimbalom.

  • Music from Hajdusag
  • Mezofoldi Music
  • Szegedi Songs

Transylvania

Some of the oldest Hungarian music was preserved in the region that is now a part of Romania, sheltered by the Carpathian Mountains. As villages were separated by great distances, a multitude of music emerged. The violin is the main instrument used in these songs.

  • Historical Songs from Transylvania
  • Vajdaszentivanyi Songs
  • Mezosegi Music
